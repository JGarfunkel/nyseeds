Murals.

The Village of Ardsley recognizes the significant aesthetic and cultural value that murals bring to the community. Residents, property owners, and artists are encouraged to collaborate in the creation of public art murals that enhance the visual environment and foster community pride.

Any individual or group seeking to paint or create a mural must first obtain explicit written permission from the property owner, whether the property is privately owned or managed by a public agency. The painting of murals on public or private property without the owner's authorization is prohibited and subject to legal penalties.

The Village does not endorse or tolerate illegal graffiti or unauthorized artwork on the exterior of buildings visible to the public, regardless of its artistic content or intent. All provisions under this chapter regarding property maintenance apply to murals.

Purpose. These guidelines provide anyone who wants to install a mural with a reasonable process that safeguards both the interests of the community and those of the individual building/property owner. The guidelines are designed to assure that murals within the Village enhance the community's appearance and contribute positively to the Village's visual character, cultural enrichment, and community identity without confusing drivers and/or pedestrians or causing any other negative impact on public safety or welfare.

Location.

Murals are only permitted in the B-1 and B-2 business districts.

Murals are permitted on the side or rear walls of buildings in the Village's business districts.

Mural proposals located on NYS rights-of-way must comply with any applicable NYSDOT laws regarding visual appearance and possible effect on traffic in the area of the mural and must obtain any NYSDOT approval as may be required now or in the future. Depending on the height of the mural and proximity to the NYS Thruway, approval from the NYS Thruway Authority may be required.

All murals on privately owned properties in the Village's B-1 and B-2 Districts are subject to these guidelines. The property owner and the artists are responsible for entering into a separate agreement regarding the ownership, maintenance and removal of the art installation.

All murals on publicly owned property are subject to these guidelines. All murals on publicly owned property become the property of the Village, and the Village can remove or cover the mural at any time for any reason and without giving notice to the artist. The Village shall be the exclusive owner of the murals on publicly owned property which may be maintained, modified, repaired, covered, reproduced and used in social media and Village communications, or removed by the Village at the Village's sole discretion.

Murals on public or private properties without the Village's or property owner's consent are prohibited by law.

Mural designs and content.

Murals should bring warmth and joy to life with bold uplifting colors. Murals can only include designs celebrating themes in nature, flowers, animals, sea life, lush plants, cultural/historical elements of the Village, abstract or geometric forms.

Murals cannot obscure windows or entranceways.

Murals shall not include:

Text, symbols, or designs that serve as commercial messaging, advertising, or branding.

Moving parts.

Illumination. No flashing or moving lights are permitted. No internal illumination is allowed.

Obscene content. For purposes of this subsection, any material is obscene if applying contemporary community standards:

The predominant appeal is to prurient interest in sex; and

The average person would find the material depicts or describes sexual content in a patently offensive way; and

A reasonable person would find the material lacks serious literary, artistic, political, or scientific value.

A mural containing any image, logo, slogan, or message that promotes a commercial business, product, or service, whether related to the premises or not, will be classified as signage and must comply with Village Code § 200-82C.

Application requirements.

A completed application to the Board of Trustees ("BOT").

A detailed rendering of the proposed mural, including dimensions, colors, and materials.

A statement of the mural's intent and relevance to the community character.

Timeline for project completion.

Written permission from the property owner.

Review criteria.

All proposals will be reviewed by the Ardsley Arts Commission and approved by the BOT. The BOT may approve by resolution all mural designs selected by the Arts Commission. However, approval or denial is in the sole discretion of the Board of Trustees.

In evaluating proposals for public murals, the following criteria will be applied to ensure compatibility with community goals and standards:

Aesthetically appealing. Appropriate recommended content.

Appropriateness of location. The proposed location must be suitable for a mural. Certain sites may be deemed inappropriate due to safety concerns, anticipated maintenance challenges, or the complexity of the building's architectural geometry.

Density of murals. To maintain a balanced aesthetic and avoid the appearance of blight, the number of murals within a given area may be limited. Proposals for neighborhoods with an existing concentration of murals may face greater scrutiny and may not be approved.

Scale of the mural. Murals, by nature, are large-scale works of art. The scale of the mural must be harmonious with the surrounding environment. Mural size shall be determined by the wall surface to be covered. Smaller walls may be completely covered. On large walls, murals should be large enough to dominate the wall surface but not so large as to overwhelm the local streetscape. Generally, one mural will be permitted per structure.

Mural proposals that do not meet all the design criteria/guidelines may be denied in total or accepted with required modifications.

Materials and maintenance.

Murals shall be well designed and incorporate high-quality materials that enhance the overall appearance of the site and do not adversely affect the safe and efficient movement of vehicles and pedestrians. Materials may include paint or other media appropriate for exterior use, such as tile or mosaic. Materials shall be long-lasting and graffiti-resistant to the greatest extent possible.

Colors, through vibrant, should be complementary and harmonious with the exterior colors of the building structure, as well as consistent with the chosen theme. Neon, fluorescent, or reflective-type colors or materials are discouraged.

The mural shall be designed and supervised by a qualified artist/muralist, one with sufficient knowledge and experience with the application of mural materials.

Routine maintenance of the artwork becomes the responsibility of the building owner where the artwork is located. If, for whatever reason, the mural falls into disrepair, the building owner will be notified, in writing, and required to make necessary repairs within 120 days. If the repairs are not made within the specified time, the Village reserves the right to remove the mural at the owner's expense, or cover the mural with opaque paint at the owner's expense.

If property ownership is transferred, all obligations for mural construction, maintenance and repair are assumed by the property owner.

The Village may use and/or reproduce all artwork for non-commercial purposes, both on public and private property, including, but not limited to, reproductions used in social media by the Village, as well as in Village newsletters and communications. While all reproductions by the Village will contain credit to the artist, no compensation or royalties of any nature shall be due to the artist or the property owner. This provision must appear in any written approval and agreement between the property owner and the artist.

Review criteria.

All proposals will be reviewed by the Ardsley Arts Commission and approved by the BOT. The BOT may approve by resolution all mural designs selected by the Arts Commission. However, approval or denial is in the sole discretion of the Board of Trustees.

In evaluating proposals for public murals, the following criteria will be applied to ensure compatibility with community goals and standards:

Aesthetically appealing. Appropriate recommended content.

Appropriateness of location. The proposed location must be suitable for a mural. Certain sites may be deemed inappropriate due to safety concerns, anticipated maintenance challenges, or the complexity of the building's architectural geometry.

Density of murals. To maintain a balanced aesthetic and avoid the appearance of blight, the number of murals within a given area may be limited. Proposals for neighborhoods with an existing concentration of murals may face greater scrutiny and may not be approved.

Scale of the mural. Murals, by nature, are large-scale works of art. The scale of the mural must be harmonious with the surrounding environment. Mural size shall be determined by the wall surface to be covered. Smaller walls may be completely covered. On large walls, murals should be large enough to dominate the wall surface but not so large as to overwhelm the local streetscape. Generally, one mural will be permitted per structure.

Mural proposals that do not meet all the design criteria/guidelines may be denied in total or accepted with required modifications.

Materials and maintenance.

Murals shall be well designed and incorporate high-quality materials that enhance the overall appearance of the site and do not adversely affect the safe and efficient movement of vehicles and pedestrians. Materials may include paint or other media appropriate for exterior use, such as tile or mosaic. Materials shall be long-lasting and graffiti-resistant to the greatest extent possible.

Colors, through vibrant, should be complementary and harmonious with the exterior colors of the building structure, as well as consistent with the chosen theme. Neon, fluorescent, or reflective-type colors or materials are discouraged.

The mural shall be designed and supervised by a qualified artist/muralist, one with sufficient knowledge and experience with the application of mural materials.

Routine maintenance of the artwork becomes the responsibility of the building owner where the artwork is located. If, for whatever reason, the mural falls into disrepair, the building owner will be notified, in writing, and required to make necessary repairs within 120 days. If the repairs are not made within the specified time, the Village reserves the right to remove the mural at the owner's expense, or cover the mural with opaque paint at the owner's expense.

If property ownership is transferred, all obligations for mural construction, maintenance and repair are assumed by the property owner.

The Village may use and/or reproduce all artwork for non-commercial purposes, both on public and private property, including, but not limited to, reproductions used in social media by the Village, as well as in Village newsletters and communications. While all reproductions by the Village will contain credit to the artist, no compensation or royalties of any nature shall be due to the artist or the property owner. This provision must appear in any written approval and agreement between the property owner and the artist.
